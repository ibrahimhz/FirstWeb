Short History

Syria, a small country located in the Middle East on the shore of the Mediterranean Sea is one of the oldest civilzations known to mankind. Human remains found in Syria date back approximately 700,000 years ago. Syria has been conquered by many emperors starting with Sargon. He was the first emperor in history and he conquered the Middle East. Then the Assyrian Empire into power for 2000 years. The Persian invasion took over followed by Alexander the Great. Then, the Romans conquered the land right before Syria became mostly arabic muslims. 90% of Syria became arabic muslims and this cause the Christians to start Crusades. One hundred years later, Saladin took back most of the cities taken by the crusades. After Saladin came the Mongolian invasions, then Timur came and slaughtered the people in Damascus and Aleppo. Peace came to Syria once the Ottoman Empire controlled the area for hundreds of years until World War I. The Syrian people won their independence 20 years after the war on September 28, 1961.

Fun Facts

  • The capital of Syria, Damascus, is known to be the oldest occupied city in the world.
  • Syrian cities are mentioned in multiple religious scriptures such as the Quran and Bible
  • The Palmyrene Empire in Syria was ruled by Queen Zenobia in the 3rd century AD and she was known as a fierce warrior and conquered many Roman territories.
  • The area occupied by Syria and some neighboring countries was all controlled by Syria and they were know as the "Cradle of Civilization" as many achievement such as agriculture, trade, religion and writing emerged from there.
  • Syria is also home to the oldest Byzantine Church in the world, the church of Saint Simeon Stylites, which dates back to the 5th century.

Courtyard Houses of Syria

Courtyard housing architecture dates back to the third millennium and they were an essential element of every house in Syria. The idea of the courtyard architecture came into place from the way Arab nomads first set up their tents in the desert and left space in the middle to protect their cattle. This concept grew and developed with the Syrian architecture.
